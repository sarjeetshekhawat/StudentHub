Maintaining good attendance is one of the most impactful things you can do for your academic success. Studies consistently show that students with attendance above 85% perform significantly better in exams.

## Why Attendance Matters More Than You Think

Most universities require a minimum of **75% attendance** to be eligible to sit exams. Falling below this threshold can mean:

- Being barred from final exams
- Losing internal assessment marks
- Missing critical in-class explanations that textbooks don't cover

## The 75% Rule Explained

If you have 100 total classes in a semester, you must attend at least **75 classes**. That means you can only miss 25 — that's less than one per week.

## 10 Proven Strategies to Improve Your Attendance

### 1. Track It Weekly — Not Monthly

Most students only realize they've fallen below 75% when it's too late. Check your attendance every Friday so you have time to course-correct.

### 2. Set Morning Alarms with Purpose

Instead of setting one alarm, set three: one to wake up, one to get ready, and one to leave. This prevents the "I'll skip just this once" spiral.

### 3. Study the Night Before

Students who review tomorrow's lecture notes before bed are far more motivated to attend because they understand the context.

### 4. Sit in the Front Row

This sounds simple, but students who sit in the front row are significantly less likely to skip class. Accountability is a powerful motivator.

### 5. Form an Attendance Pact with a Friend

Make a deal with a classmate: you both have to attend every class together. Social accountability is incredibly effective.

### 6. Keep a "Why I'm Here" Reminder

Write down your goal — a career, a grade, a scholarship — and read it when you don't feel like going to class.

### 7. Use the Pomodoro Technique the Night Before

If late-night procrastination is causing you to sleep in, use our [Pomodoro Focus Timer](/dashboard/focus) to study in focused 25-minute blocks.

### 8. Understand the Compound Effect

Missing one class is fine. Missing three means you've fallen behind on assignments, notes, and context — which makes the next class harder to follow, which makes you less likely to attend. Break the cycle early.

### 9. Talk to Your Professor Early

If you're struggling, approach your professor *before* your attendance drops below 75%, not after. Professors are usually more accommodating with students who are proactive.

### 10. Use an Attendance Tracker

Our [dashboard attendance tracker](/dashboard/attendance) lets you add all your subjects and monitor your percentage in real time. You'll always know exactly where you stand.

## What to Do If You're Already Below 75%

Don't panic. Here's your action plan:

1. **Calculate the damage**: Use the [attendance calculator](/attendance-calculator) to see how many classes you need to attend.
2. **Talk to your professor**: Explain your situation honestly.
3. **Request medical documentation**: If you missed classes due to illness, submit documentation immediately.
4. **Attend every remaining class**: Don't miss a single one — each class now counts double.

## Final Thoughts

Attendance isn't just about rules — it's about showing up for yourself. The habits you build in college carry into your career. Start treating every class as a non-negotiable appointment.
