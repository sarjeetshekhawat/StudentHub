Your GPA (Grade Point Average) is one of the most important numbers in your academic career. It affects scholarships, graduate school admissions, and job applications. This guide explains everything.

## What Is a GPA?

GPA stands for **Grade Point Average**. It measures your academic performance across all courses, weighted by the credit hours of each course.

The most common scale used globally is the **4.0 scale**:

| Grade | Points |
|-------|--------|
| A+ / A | 4.0 |
| A- | 3.7 |
| B+ | 3.3 |
| B  | 3.0 |
| B- | 2.7 |
| C+ | 2.3 |
| C  | 2.0 |
| D  | 1.0 |
| F  | 0.0 |

## How GPA Is Calculated

The formula is straightforward:

**GPA = Total (Grade Points × Credits) ÷ Total Credits**

### Example

Let's say you have three subjects:

| Subject | Credits | Grade | Points |
|---------|---------|-------|--------|
| Mathematics | 4 | A (4.0) | 16 |
| Physics | 3 | B+ (3.3) | 9.9 |
| English | 2 | A- (3.7) | 7.4 |

**Total Credits** = 4 + 3 + 2 = **9**  
**Total Points** = 16 + 9.9 + 7.4 = **33.3**  
**GPA** = 33.3 ÷ 9 = **3.70**

## What's a Good GPA?

| GPA Range | Standing |
|-----------|----------|
| 3.7 – 4.0 | Excellent / Dean's List |
| 3.3 – 3.6 | Very Good |
| 3.0 – 3.2 | Good |
| 2.5 – 2.9 | Average |
| Below 2.5 | Below Average |

Most graduate programs require a minimum GPA of **3.0**, and competitive programs often prefer **3.5+**.

## CGPA vs GPA: What's the Difference?

- **GPA** is your grade point average for a *single semester*.
- **CGPA** (Cumulative GPA) is your grade point average across *all semesters*.

## 7 Strategies to Raise Your GPA

### 1. Prioritize High-Credit Courses

A B in a 4-credit course hurts more than a B in a 1-credit course. Focus your energy where it mathematically matters most.

### 2. Don't Chase Perfection — Chase Balance

It's better to get A- in all courses than A in two and C in the rest.

### 3. Attend Every Lecture

Students who attend all lectures typically score half a letter grade higher than those who don't. Use our [attendance tracker](/dashboard/attendance) to stay on top of this.

### 4. Use Spaced Repetition

Instead of cramming the night before, review material in short sessions spread over weeks. This technique is proven to dramatically improve retention.

### 5. Form Study Groups

Teaching a concept to someone else is the fastest way to master it yourself. Form a group of 3-4 students per subject.

### 6. Use Office Hours

Professors give hints, explain exam formats, and clarify confusing concepts during office hours. These 30-minute sessions can be worth more than 3 hours of solo study.

### 7. Track Progress Weekly

Knowledge of how you're performing motivates you to improve. Use our [dashboard](/dashboard) to monitor all your academic metrics in one place.

## Improving a Low GPA

If your GPA is below where you'd like it, here's a recovery strategy:

1. **Identify your weak subjects** using your grade history.
2. **Retake courses where possible** — some institutions replace the old grade.
3. **Take easier electives strategically** to raise your overall average.
4. **Get consistent** — even getting all Bs from now on will steadily raise your CGPA over time.

## Conclusion

Your GPA is a reflection of your habits, not your intelligence. With the right tools and strategies, every student can improve their academic standing. Start by calculating exactly where you are today.
