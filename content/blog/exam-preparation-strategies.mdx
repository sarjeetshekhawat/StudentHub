The difference between a student who panics before exams and one who feels confident comes down to one thing: a system. This guide gives you a proven, week-by-week preparation strategy for any exam.

## Why Most Students Fail to Prepare Effectively

The typical student pattern:
- Weeks 1–3: Ignore the exam
- Week 4: Mild panic, some studying
- Day before: All-night cramming
- Exam day: Exhausted and anxious

This is backwards. Here's the right approach.

## The 4-Week Exam Preparation Framework

### Week 4 (First Week After Announcement): Understand the Landscape

**Goal**: Know exactly what you need to study before you start studying.

#### Day 1–2: Gather Your Resources
- Collect all lecture notes, slides, past papers, and reading lists
- Identify gaps where you missed lectures or didn't take notes
- Download previous years' exam papers (these are gold)

#### Day 3–4: Create a Subject Map
Mind-map every major topic in the syllabus. Don't study yet — just understand the full scope.

#### Day 5–7: Plan Your Schedule
Use our [study planner](/dashboard/tasks) to break the syllabus into daily chunks over the next 3 weeks. Be realistic — plan for 2-3 hours of focused study per day, not 8.

**Add your exam to our [Exam Countdown tracker](/dashboard/exams)** so you always know exactly how many days you have left.

---

### Week 3: Master the Fundamentals

**Goal**: Understand all concepts at a foundational level.

#### Focus Areas
- Core theory and definitions
- Key formulas and their derivations
- Worked examples from lecture notes

#### Study Technique: Feynman Method
For each topic, close your notes and explain the concept aloud as if teaching someone else. Where you struggle is where you need to spend more time.

#### Daily Schedule
- **Morning (90 min)**: Tackle the hardest topic of the day (when your brain is fresh)
- **Afternoon (60 min)**: Review morning material with active recall
- **Evening (30 min)**: Light review of the week's topics using flashcards

---

### Week 2: Deep Practice

**Goal**: Apply your knowledge under exam-like conditions.

#### The Practice Paper Strategy
1. Attempt past exam questions **without your notes**
2. Time yourself strictly
3. Mark your own work
4. For every wrong answer, trace *why* you got it wrong (concept gap, careless error, or exam technique issue)

#### Common Topics to Identify
After doing 3-4 past papers, you'll notice certain topics appear every year. These are your highest-priority areas.

#### Review and Spaced Repetition
Begin revising Week 3 material on different days to reinforce memory through spaced repetition.

---

### Week 1 (Final Week): Consolidation and Confidence

**Goal**: Sharpen what you know. Do not try to learn new material.

#### Day 7–5 Before Exam
- Complete one full past paper per day under strict exam conditions
- Review only your weak areas
- Create a "cheat sheet" summary of the most critical formulas and concepts (this process helps you memorize them)

#### Day 4–2 Before Exam
- Light review of your summary sheet
- Focus on sleep and exercise
- Avoid introducing new topics
- Practice relaxation techniques

#### Day Before Exam
- **Morning**: Light review of your cheat sheet only
- **Afternoon**: Do something relaxing — walk, watch a show, play a game
- **Evening**: Prepare everything (pens, calculator, ID, water)
- **Bedtime**: 8 hours of sleep. Non-negotiable. Sleep is when your brain consolidates everything you've studied.

#### Exam Morning
- Wake up with enough time to eat breakfast (proven to improve cognitive performance)
- Light review of your cheat sheet (15 minutes max)
- Arrive 10 minutes early to settle your nerves

## Exam Day Techniques

### The First 5 Minutes Are Critical
Read the entire paper before writing a single word. This helps your brain start processing all questions simultaneously.

### Answer the Easy Questions First
Build confidence and bank marks before tackling harder questions.

### Allocate Time Per Question
Divide your time by the marks available. If a 10-mark question gets 10% of the marks, give it 10% of your time.

### Don't Leave Blanks
A structured, partially correct answer often earns more marks than a blank. Write what you know, show your working, and explain your reasoning.

## Managing Exam Anxiety

1. **Prepare thoroughly** — anxiety decreases proportionally with preparation
2. **Exercise daily** — even 20 minutes of walking reduces cortisol levels significantly
3. **Use breathing techniques** — 4-second inhale, 4-second hold, 6-second exhale calms the nervous system in minutes
4. **Accept imperfection** — you don't need a perfect score; you need a passing grade, and you can improve from there
